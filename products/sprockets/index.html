Fitment Guide

Front sprocket, rear sprocket, and 520 chain decisions.

Motocross sprocket fitment is more than matching a tooth count. Chain pitch, countershaft pattern, rear hub interface, bolt circle, center bore, and model-year fitment all need to line up before a sprocket can be produced or purchased with confidence.

Front

Countershaft changes feel bigger.

A front sprocket change can make a bike feel shorter or taller quickly. Fitment must confirm the countershaft pattern, chain pitch, and clearance around the case and chain guide.

Rear

Rear teeth tune response.

Rear sprocket tooth count is often used to fine-tune corner drive, launch feel, and track response. Rear fitment must match hub geometry, bolt circle, center bore, and chain pitch.

520

Chain pitch must match.

A 520 chain motocross sprocket is only correct when the bike application, chain pitch, and tooth count target match the verified Langbiang Gravity fitment path.

How should riders choose front vs rear sprocket changes?

Front sprocket changes create a larger gearing shift. Rear sprocket changes are better for fine tuning drive feel, torque delivery, and track response.

Can the finder search by make, model, year, and type?

Yes. The dedicated finder app handles make, model, year, and model type search, then routes fitment support through the Langbiang Gravity sprocket workflow.

Does public SKU guidance expose private geometry?

No. Public LBG SKUs help riders and dealers discuss applications. Private geometry remains part of the protected front and rear sprocket verification process.
