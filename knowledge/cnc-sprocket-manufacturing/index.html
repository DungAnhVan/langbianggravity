CNC sprocket manufacturing

CNC helps control sprocket geometry, but it is only one part of the process. Blank quality, tooling, heat treatment, and inspection still determine the finished part.

Definition

CNC is the machining step that shapes the part from a controlled program, keeping critical dimensions consistent across a batch.

Practical use

It is useful for private geometry, custom tooth counts, and repeatable batch work where fitment must stay consistent.

Material notes

Steel and aluminium need different toolpaths and finishing. Tooth form, runout, and inspection matter more than the process label alone.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming CNC automatically means correct fitment.
  • Skipping heat treatment or surface finish checks.
  • Ignoring chain pitch, bolt circle, and center bore in the drawing stage.
