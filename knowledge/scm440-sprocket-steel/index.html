SCM440 sprocket steel

SCM440 is a chromium-molybdenum alloy steel used for tough, heat-treatable parts. For sprockets, it is chosen when wear resistance and impact tolerance matter.

Definition

SCM440 is a JIS alloy steel family used in applications that need strength, toughness, and controlled heat treatment.

Practical use

It fits hard-use sprocket programs where a buyer wants a durable steel option for street, off-road, or fleet use.

Material notes

Finished performance depends on heat treatment, tooth profile, finish, and inspection, not the steel name alone.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming every alloy steel behaves the same after machining.
  • Ignoring hardness targets and surface finish after heat treatment.
  • Using the raw material name as a substitute for finished-part specification.
